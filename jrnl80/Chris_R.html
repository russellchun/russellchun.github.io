Long Island Ducks Sign Former Mets All-Star

By Christopher Rosvoglou & Nick Faria

With the Ducks gearing up for the 2018 season, Long Island baseball will witness a familiar face in the bullpen. Although he hasn't played professional baseball in New York since 2011, Francisco Rodriguez is ready to return.

sources: K-Rod has an agreement to pitch for the Long Island Ducks and is on his way to L.I.

— Jon Heyman (@JonHeyman) April 29, 2018

That's right Ducks fans, Francisco 'K-Rod' Rodriguez has agreed to terms with the Long Island Ducks. At 36 years old, Rodriguez will have his chance to revitalize his career in the Atlantic League of Professional Baseball

The Accolades

  • 6x MLB All-Star
  • World Series Champion (2002)
  • 2x AL Rolaids Relief Man Award
  • 4th most saves all-time (437)

Ducks Preident/GM Michael Pfaff expressed his excitement over the addition, telling Newsday â€œThe goal is to get him in here as soon as possible. We anticipate his arrival this week." After recently landing former All-Star Jair Jurrjens, Pfaff is building a formidable pitching staff for the Ducks, which currently features five former MLB pitchers.

With the home-opener taking place on May 4, Central Islip will experience the charisma and talent that Mets fans witnessed for three seasons.

Reactions to the Signing

"Having the privilege to work alongside Francisco Rodriguez is like a dream come true," Peter LoDuca, Ducks bullpen catcher said."I grew up watching K-Rod pitch for the Mets, so actually being a part of his amazing career is an honor." As for the fans gearing up to see K-Rod in action, well they're definitely excited:"It's been five years since our last league championship, but I fully expect our revamped bullpen to give us the edge in any matchup" Charlie Maisano, Long Island Ducks fanatic said. "K-Rod might be out of his prime, yet he's still capable of closing at a very high level for the Atlantic League."

K-Rod on his Comeback

Francisco Rodriguez expressed his desire to keep his playing career going, telling MyCentralJersey.com that he's on a mission to make Bethpage Ballpark his new home. "I can guarantee I'm going to try and put on a show for them, and hopefully they'll enjoy it. I'm going to try as hard as I can to get back."
